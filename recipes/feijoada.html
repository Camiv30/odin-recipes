Feijoada

This feijoada recipe is a version of a traditional Brazilian black bean stew that maintains the rich smoky flavors. Additional meats, including sausage may be added if disired. This is excellent served over some white rice.

Prep time: 4 hrs

Servings: 8 servings

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon of olive oil
  • 1 1/2 cups of chopped onions
  • 1/2 cup of green onions
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1 (12 ounce) packege of dry black beans, soaked overnight
  • 2 smoked ham hocks
  • 8 ounces of diced ham
  • 1/2 of tickly sliced bacon, diced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1/8 tablespoon of ground coriander
  • 1/2 cup of chopped fresh cilantro
  • 1/2 cup of chopped fresh parsley

Directions

  1. Heat oil in a large pot or dutch oven. add 3/4 cup of chopped onions, green onions, and garlic; cook and stir well until softened, about 4 minutes. Pour soaked beans and fill enough water to cover beans by 3 inches. Bring to boil, then reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer uncovered for 2 hours, or until tender.
  2. While beans are cooking, place the ham hocks in a smaller pot with 1/4 cup of chopped onions. Cover with water and simmer until meat pulls of the bone easily, about 1 hour. Drain and add it to the beans.
  3. Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 375 degrees F.
  4. Place ham, bacon, and remaining onion in a baking dish. Bake until mixture is crispy, 15 minutes.
  5. Drain bacon and ham mixture and add it to beans. Season it with bay leaves, coriander, salt, and pepper. Simmer, uncovered, for 30 minutes.
  6. Stir in chopped cilantro and parsley just before serving.
